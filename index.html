How reports will be verified

Our planned
verification process.

We take accuracy seriously — especially for health-sensitive information about schools, parks, travel, and other spaces families trust. Getting verification right takes time to do properly. Here's the process we are designing, informed by conversations with allergists, parents, and patient safety experts.

These features are in development. We are being transparent about what is planned rather than implying it is already live.

🔨 In development
🔐
Identity Verification
Planned email + phone verification for every reviewer, with automated detection of suspicious activity patterns before any review is published.
🔨 In development
🏷️
Allergen Tag Consistency
Planned moderation checks to ensure allergen tags accurately match review content — preventing accidental or misleading labelling.
🔨 In development
🔄
Community Cross-Referencing
Once sufficient reviews exist, conflicting reports will trigger alerts and community flags so users always have the full picture.
🤝 Seeking advisors
🏥
Specialist Advisory Panel
We plan to work with board-certified allergists and registered dietitians to review our tagging standards and advise on edge cases.
📋
A note on our current stage: Allergy Ally is in early development. Verification systems described above are planned features, not yet live. During early access, we will be transparent about what checks are in place at each stage. We will never represent unverified data as verified. If you have expertise in allergy safety or medical information verification and would like to contribute to our advisory process, we'd love to hear from you.
The vision
